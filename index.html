How Solar Energy works

Solar energy works by capturing the sun’s energy using photovoltaic (PV) cells in the solar panels. These cells are made up of semiconducting materials like silicon, which absorb the sunlight and convert it into electricity. The PV cells are connected to an inverter, which converts the direct current (DC) electricity produced by the solar cells into the alternating current (AC) electricity used in homes and businesses. The electricity is then sent to the electrical grid or stored in batteries for later use. Solar energy systems can be installed on rooftops or in open areas and can be used to power homes, businesses, and even remote areas. Solar energy systems can also be combined with other energy sources, such as wind or geothermal, to form hybrid systems. Energy solutions are recommended based on appliances to be powered.
